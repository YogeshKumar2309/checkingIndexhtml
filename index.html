Here's a time-line of his Life...

Kalam was a well-known Indian scientist and politician who was awarded the Bharat Ratna in 1997, the Padma Vibhushan in 1990, and the Padma Bhushan in 2011. He was known as the "Missile Man of India" and the "People's President"

  • Birth:Born Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam on October 15, 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • Education:Graduated from Saint Joseph's College in 1954 with a degree in Physics, and from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1960 with a degree in Aeronautical Engineering
  • Joined DRDO: Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1958
  • Joined ISRO:Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1969
  • SLV-III:Led the development of the SLV-III, India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, which put the Rohini satellite into orbit in 1980
  • Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme:Chief of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme, responsible for the development of the AGNI and PRITHVI missiles
  • Pokhran II nuclear tests: Chief project coordinator for the Pokhran II nuclear tests in May 1998
  • President of India:Elected as the 11th president of India in 2002 and served until 2007
  • Death: Died on July 27, 2015 after collapsing during a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management in Shillong
